The Merneptah Stele

c. 1208 BCE

The first known mention of “Israel” is found on an Egyptian victory monument by Pharaoh Merneptah. It declares: “Israel is laid waste, its seed is no more.”

Ashkelon and the Philistines

c. 1150 BCE

Excavations at Ashkelon reveal European ancestry among the Philistines, suggesting a migration from the Aegean world into the Levant during the Late Bronze Age collapse.

The House of David Inscription

c. 840 BCE

The Tel Dan Stele, discovered in northern Israel, references the “House of David,” offering archaeological support for the historical King David mentioned in the Bible.
